Week 2: Improving the game

Monday, Jun 15, 2020| Tags: outdoor, sports, tennis

Day 8 of tennis

Day 8 of formal tennis on June 15, 2020. Following are the improvements from last week:

  1. Better judgement of the ball: The best position for a forehand or backhand is the max height of ball at the abdomen level provides ideal control and swing. The ball position with the right tilted face and lower to upper swing of the racquet generates the right momentum for the ball to cross the net.
  2. Hitting the ball deeper in the court: As a trick in the game, the right spot to hit the ball is as deep as possible in the opponent’s court. This would yield lesser space for them to adjust or they would require more time to adjust, causing forced errors.
  3. Fixing weak areas needs work: My back hand still needs work. I am still struggling with the two-hand backhand. However, on the 8th day, I am still improving my single handed back hand return. I have less control on where the ball lands on the other side of the net, but atleast is within the lines and crosses cleanly across the net.
  4. Balancing your game: The focus on my game is important; at times, the need to play with the right technique causes me to be up-tight. Trying to focus on playing my natural game while staying with the technique helps. Too much calculations and alertness destroys the enjoyment. After all, one plays everyday to improve.
  5. Learning with rallies: The coach is encouraging me to play rallies so that I can learn with a full court game. It is still a 50-50 game, not the right level tet.
