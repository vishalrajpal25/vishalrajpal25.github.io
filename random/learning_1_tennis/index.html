Learnings 1: For the love of tennis

Monday, Jun 8, 2020| Tags: outdoor, sports, tennis

I am at my fourth day of formal tennis learning on June 8, 2020. While the first day gives you a lot of confidence as you start hitting the ball, it is not soon that you realize in the 3rd or 4th day the importance of technique in the game.

Below are the 4 key learnings that I got for a beginner level tennis player:

  1. Stance: The key element to understand both fore-hand and back-hand hitting in tennis is the stance or the body position wrt to the ball. It is critical to take the right stance on each shot. The overall body faces the ball in both the shots and it requires one to be ready in the stance as the ball enters the good side of the court.

  2. Distance between the ball and body: This is another element to having better control in each shot. The closer the ball is to the body while contact is achieved, the harder would be to hit a good shot in return. Again, the practice calls for understanding the ball’s speed, swing and elevation at it will come closer to one’s contact helps identify the apt distance between the body and the ball. One needs to identify the right distance based on the position of contact.

  3. Timing: I struggled a bit with getting the ball timing right. The time to hit the ball is not driven by the anxiety to parcel the ball back, but with the right time at which it needs to be sent. Wait for the ball to land, let is reach it’s right bounce height and swing the racquet for the optimum shot.

  4. Power vs control: The desire to hit a power shot causes the loss of control. The urge is bigger and bigger with each day, but one needs restraint. Control is more important than power. Once there is control, power will follow through with practice.
